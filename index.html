About the Journal

Journal of Regional Anesthesia & Ultrasound (JRAU) is an international, peer-reviewed, open-access journal dedicated to regional anesthesia, ultrasound-guided procedures, perioperative pain medicine, and innovation in anesthetic practice.

JRAU provides a focused platform for clinicians, researchers, educators, and trainees working in ultrasound-guided regional anesthesia and related perioperative fields.

Original Research
Performance of Large Language Models in ASA Physical Status Classification Using Expert-Validated Synthetic Preoperative Scenarios: A Comparative Study of GPT-4o and Gemini 2.0
Rasim Onur Karaoğlu, MD · Açelya Toprak Karaoğlu · Sezen Kumaş Solak, MD · Serdar Demirgan, MD PhD
Department of Anesthesiology and Reanimation, Bağcılar Training and Research Hospital, Istanbul, Turkey
Background: The ASA Physical Status classification is widely used for preoperative risk stratification but suffers from substantial inter-rater variability. This study evaluates GPT-4o and Gemini 2.0 Flash on 487 expert-validated synthetic preoperative scenarios.

Conclusions: Both GPT-4o (κw=0.936) and Gemini 2.0 Flash (κw=0.933) achieved almost perfect agreement against expert consensus, matching the expert panel's own inter-rater reliability. Prospective clinical validation is the logical next step.

Case Report
Anesthetic Management in a Patient with Huntington's Chorea: A Case Report
Sezen Kumaş Solak, MD
Department of Anesthesiology and Reanimation, Bağcılar Training and Research Hospital, Istanbul, Turkey
Background: Huntington's chorea (HC) is a rare autosomal dominant neurodegenerative disorder characterized by progressive choreiform movements, cognitive decline, and psychiatric disturbances. The perioperative management of patients with HC poses significant challenges, particularly due to pharyngeal muscle involvement, increased aspiration risk, and altered responses to anesthetic agents.

Conclusions: Awake fiberoptic intubation combined with propofol-remifentanil TIVA and low-dose rocuronium under BIS and TOF monitoring provides a safe and effective anesthetic approach in patients with Huntington's chorea.
