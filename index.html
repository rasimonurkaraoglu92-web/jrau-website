About the Journal

Journal of Regional Anesthesia & Ultrasound (JRAU) is an international, peer-reviewed, open-access journal dedicated to regional anesthesia, ultrasound-guided procedures, perioperative pain medicine, and innovation in anesthetic practice.

JRAU provides a focused platform for clinicians, researchers, educators, and trainees working in ultrasound-guided regional anesthesia and related perioperative fields.

Ahead of Print

AHEAD OF PRINT
A Radiative Forcing-Based Life Cycle Assessment Model for Inhaled Anaesthetic Agents: Integrating Climate and Ecotoxicity Metrics
Rasim Onur Karaoğlu, MD
Department of Anesthesiology and Reanimation, SBÜ Bağcılar Training and Research Hospital, İstanbul, Turkey
Background: We propose a novel modelling framework that derives instantaneous radiative forcing (RF) directly from clinical input parameters for sevoflurane, desflurane, and isoflurane.

Conclusions: RF per MAC-hour provides a physically transparent alternative to GWP for comparing the climate footprint of inhaled anaesthetics. Desflurane generates 5.2-fold more RF per MAC-hour than sevoflurane.

Publication Ethics

JRAU follows international standards for publication ethics, peer review, authorship transparency, conflict of interest disclosure, data integrity, and responsible use of artificial intelligence in scientific writing.

All submitted manuscripts are expected to comply with ethical principles including appropriate ethics committee approval, informed consent when applicable, plagiarism avoidance, and transparent reporting.
