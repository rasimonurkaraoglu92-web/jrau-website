About the Journal

Journal of Regional Anesthesia & Ultrasound (JRAU) is an international, peer-reviewed, open-access journal dedicated to regional anesthesia, ultrasound-guided procedures, perioperative pain medicine, and innovation in anesthetic practice.

JRAU aims to provide a focused platform for clinicians, researchers, educators, and trainees working in ultrasound-guided regional anesthesia and related perioperative fields.

A Radiative Forcing-Based Life Cycle Assessment Model for Inhaled Anaesthetic Agents: Integrating Climate and Ecotoxicity Metrics
Rasim Onur Karaoğlu, MD
Department of Anesthesiology and Reanimation, SBÜ Bağcılar Training and Research Hospital, İstanbul, Turkey
Background: The environmental impact of inhaled anaesthetics is conventionally quantified using the 100-year global warming potential (GWP₁₀₀). We propose a novel modelling framework that derives instantaneous radiative forcing (RF, mW·m⁻²) directly from clinical input parameters for sevoflurane, desflurane, and isoflurane.

Results: At standard clinical conditions, desflurane generates 5.2-fold more RF per MAC-hour than sevoflurane. The GWP₁₀₀-based ratio is 61.8-fold, suggesting the conventional metric overestimates the relative impact of desflurane by a factor of approximately 12.

Conclusions: RF per MAC-hour provides a physically transparent alternative to GWP for comparing the climate footprint of inhaled anaesthetics.
